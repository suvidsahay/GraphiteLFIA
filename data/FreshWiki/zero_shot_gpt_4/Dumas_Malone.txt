# Dumas Malone

Dumas Malone (January 10, 1892 – December 27, 1986) was an American historian, biographer, and editor of distinction. He is most famously known for his six-volume biography of Thomas Jefferson, "Jefferson and His Time," which is considered one of the most thorough accounts of a U.S president’s life. 

## Early Life and Education

### Family Background
Born in Coldwater, Mississippi, Malone hailed from a modest background. His father was a country merchant, while his mother was a dedicated homemaker who took keen interest in the young Malone's early education.

### Primary Education
Malone's primary education was predominantly influenced by the southern rural environment in which he grew up. At a young age, he developed a love for literature and history, spurred on by his mother's passion for education. 

### College Years
After high school, Malone attended Emory College (now Emory University) in Georgia. There, he excelled academically, demonstrating a deep love for history and literature that carried him to the top of his class. He graduated in 1910 with a Bachelor of Arts degree.

### Higher Education
Following his college education, Malone attended Yale University, where he obtained his Ph.D. in History in 1916. His doctoral dissertation on the abolition of slavery was an indication of his keen interest in American history and civil rights.

## Career

### Early Career
Malone started his career as an instructor in history at Yale University after completing his Ph.D. After a few years, he advanced to the position of assistant professor, before taking a brief hiatus to serve in the U.S. Navy during World War I. After the war, Malone resumed his position at Yale before moving to the University of Virginia in 1923, where he served as a professor of history.

### The Jefferson Project
In 1939, Malone embarked on the monumental task of writing a definitive biography of Thomas Jefferson. Titled "Jefferson and His Time," the six-volume work spanned over 40 years of Malone’s life. He meticulously researched every aspect of Jefferson's life, from his early years to his presidency and retirement. The biography is considered one of the most in-depth studies of a U.S president's life and has gained widespread acclaim for its thoroughness and academic rigor.

### Later Career
After the completion of the Jefferson Project, Malone continued his research and teaching career. He served as the director of Harvard University Press and was a member of several history and literature associations.

## Achievements

### Awards and Honors
Malone received numerous awards and honors throughout his career. Notably, he won the 1975 Pulitzer Prize for History for the fifth volume of "Jefferson and His Time." He also received the Presidential Medal of Freedom in 1981, a testament to his significant contributions to the field of history.

### Books and Publications
Aside from his magnum opus on Jefferson, Malone authored several other books and scholarly articles. His works include "The Public Life of Thomas Cooper," "Dictionary of American Biography," and "The American Republic," among others.

## Personal Life

### Marriage and Family
Malone was married to Elisabeth Gifford, a woman of keen intellect who supported his career and shared his love for literature and history. The couple had two daughters, who inherited their parents' love for education.

### Hobbies and Interests
Beyond his scholarly work, Malone had a deep appreciation for nature and gardening. He was an avid reader and enjoyed traveling, especially to historic sites related to his research.

## Death and Legacy

### Last Years and Death
Malone continued his scholarly activities well into his 90s. He died on December 27, 1986, at the age of 94. His death was mourned by the academic community, which lost one of its most respected and dedicated scholars.

### Legacy
Malone's legacy is marked by his unwavering commitment to rigorous historical research and his monumental work on Thomas Jefferson. His scholarly contributions greatly expanded our understanding of American history, making him one of the most influential historians of the 20th century.
